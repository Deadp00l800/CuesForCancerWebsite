Our Story, Mission & Programs

Our Story

Founded from lived experience

Cues for Cancer Inc. was founded by Darrell Wood, a cancer survivor who was diagnosed with testicular cancer at just eighteen years old. That experience revealed a gap: patients and families needed more than clinical care — they needed community, creativity, and a sense of identity beyond diagnosis. Cues for Cancer was built to fill that gap, starting in the theatre community and growing into a nationwide network of support.

Empowering Lives Through Every Cue

Mission & programs

We create meaningful impact through the power of community, creativity, and connection — supporting those affected by cancer through engaging events, performances, and collaborative experiences.

Community Support Events

Gatherings — from theatre nights to community picnics — that bring survivors, families, and neighbors together so no one faces cancer alone.

Creative Spark Workshops

Theatre, music, and art-based workshops that give participants of all ages a creative outlet through diagnosis, treatment, and recovery.

Fundraising Initiatives

Ticketed performances and giving campaigns that directly fund support for families navigating the cost of a cancer journey.

Volunteer Opportunities

Meaningful ways for performers, healthcare professionals, and neighbors to lend their time and talent to our mission.

Frequently Asked Questions

Good to know

Is Cues for Cancer a registered nonprofit?

Yes — Cues for Cancer Inc. is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Norfolk, Virginia.

Who can participate in your programs?

Our events and workshops are open to individuals and families affected by cancer, regardless of where they are in their journey, from anywhere in the country.

How can my company get involved?

We partner with businesses through sponsorships, in-kind support, and co-hosted events. Visit our Corporate Partners page to learn more.
